## Behavioral Patterns

Four signal-rich patterns emerged from this period's visitor journeys:

**1. Progressive category discovery.** One visitor's session moved Trails → Galleries → Eat/Drink. They arrived with outdoor intent, shifted toward culture, then asked for dining recommendations. This cross-category exploration is precisely the multi-faceted trip planning the platform is designed to support.

**2. Map is contextual, not navigational.** Only 1 map marker click across 95 page views. Four detail opens came from the list/card interface, not the map. Visitors are using the map as a spatial overview, then drilling into venues through the sidebar — a mobile-friendly pattern consistent with iOS browsing behavior.

**3. Tight event-to-conversion funnel.** Spring Street Swing Out shows the template: tonight filter → event appears in results → detail panel opened → outbound ticket click. This is the clearest conversion path in the data and sets the bar for what "a working funnel" looks like.

**4. Chatbot positions dining as the entry point.** Both chatbot queries were about food. Visitors arriving with a specific question ask the AI for help with dining first, before anything else. The AI Concierge is functioning as a restaurant recommender — which is the right entry point for evening visitors making real-time decisions.

## Case Study: LeeAnn Brook Gallery

### One Session. Five Discoveries.

A visitor arrived at the platform with outdoor recreation intent. Over approximately 15 minutes, their session moved through five distinct discovery phases:

1. **Trails filter** — outdoor interest, looking for hiking
2. **Galleries & Museums filter** — cultural shift, exploring adjacent interests
3. **Chatbot query** — *"I came here for hiking but want to discover more — what's good for live music and galleries?"*
4. **AI response** — 5 specific recommendations, including LeeAnn Brook Gallery as the primary art venue
5. **Deep link click** — clicked `LeeAnn Brook Gallery` from chatbot response
6. **Detail panel opened** — reviewed gallery info: phone, website, Google Maps link
7. **Navigation to itineraries.html** — continued journey to curated "Perfect Day" itinerary

### What This Demonstrates

This visitor converted from "hiker" to "art enthusiast" in a single session, using the platform as both a discovery tool and a planning tool. The chatbot deep link was the trigger for the conversion. No outbound ticket purchase was tracked, but the intent to visit the gallery was unambiguous.

> **"This single session justifies the entire investment."** This is the best-case scenario for the platform: visitor arrives with one interest, discovers adjacent interests, AI cross-sells related experiences, venue gets direct traffic, and itineraries get discovered. Scaling this behavior is the goal for Q2.

## What This Means

### Wins This Month ✅

- **Direct referral to Spring Street Swing Out:** 2 confirmed ticket site clicks from 5 visitors. If those were 2 separate people who each bought a $20 ticket, the platform generated **$40 in tracked event revenue** — an **$8/visitor referral value**, before counting chatbot recommendations, the VRBO click, or the gallery discovery.
- **Chatbot proving valuable for dining:** 2 queries, 14 venue recommendations. The AI Concierge is actively positioning local restaurants to visitors before they've even left the planning phase.
- **Trip planner archetype confirmed:** The VRBO click + multi-day category exploration shows out-of-town visitors are using the map for serious trip research.
- **High engagement per visitor:** 3m 14s avg session + 9.1% bounce rate. Visitors who find the platform stay and use it.

### Opportunities 🎯

- **Chatbot deep links are invisible:** AI recommended 14 venues across 2 queries, but we have **zero click-through tracking** on those recommendations. This is the highest-volume referral channel by count and we can't measure it. Instrument chatbot deep link clicks (Phase 6 priority).
- **Gallery engagement is soft:** 1 gallery detail open despite 2 Galleries filter uses. Gallery detail panels may need stronger CTAs — visible hours, admission cost, and website link above the fold.
- **Itinerary page is invisible from Hub:** Only 1 visitor found itineraries.html. The curated routes exist but aren't surfaced from the main experience. Add "Suggested Itineraries" CTA or carousel to the Hub.
