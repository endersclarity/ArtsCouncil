48 Hours in Grass Valley & Nevada City

Two days. Two towns. Everything you need to know.

Share X FB

It was a moment that locals and frequent visitors already knew about: the Grass Valley–Nevada City Cultural District has a long history of being at the epicenter of arts, culture, and technology in California. From one of the greatest American poets, Gary Snyder, to progressive journalist Jennie Carter; filmmakers Jonathan Dayton and Adrian Molina to professional skateboarders and snowboarders—this place breeds creatives.

Maybe it's the vibes from the granite and gold quartz reverberating all around, or the sounds of the whispering pines, or the sheer number of people making art professionally and for fun. Art doesn't just grow here. It thrives and flourishes.

Friday Evening

Arrive in Grass Valley

From the moment you step out onto Mill Street in the heart of Grass Valley, you are surrounded by charming shops, an array of dining options, and public art on every corner. The iconic spire of the Del Oro Theatre can be seen at various downtown vantage points, while stunning large-scale murals tell unique local stories: the open "Mine Shaft" by John Pugh, the iconic Grass Valley sign by Justin Lovato, "A New Dawn" by Miles Toland, and the Nisenan mural "Solim Ni—I Sing" by Nikila Badua.

Finish the evening with a hand-crafted, custom-blended cocktail at the Iron Door Saloon at the beautifully renovated historic Holbrooke Hotel. Or enjoy some live music, comedy, dance, or theater at the Center for the Arts.

Saturday Morning

Downtown Grass Valley

Check out local art and artisans at Artworks Gallery or Make Local Habit. Pick up that impossible-to-find LP from Clocktower Records or Ron's Real Records, your next favorite page-turning novel at The Bookseller or something more obscure at Booktown Books, cables for your guitar amp at Foggy Mountain Music, or specialty yarn at Heathered Yarn Co.

Sip local and regional wines at Cork 49, New West Wines, or Sierra Starr Winery, or grab a beer at Grass Valley Brewery. Maybe it's something sweet you're craving: satisfy your sweet tooth with a French pastry from Cake, hand-dipped Lazy Dog Ice Cream, or something delicious and gluten-free from Corvus Bakery.

Saturday Afternoon

Empire Mine State Historic Park

Pick up lunch or a picnic from Backporch Market, Tess' Kitchen, or El Barrio Market before heading over to Empire Mine State Historic Park. Just over one mile from downtown Grass Valley, Empire Mine is the site of one of the oldest, deepest, and richest gold mines in California. Tour the lush grounds or take a more vigorous hike along its many trails.

Saturday Evening

Cross Over to Nevada City

Just four miles away is Nevada City, originally named "Nevada" (Spanish for "snow-covered") after a particularly snowy winter in 1850. Historic Victorian homes and their heritage gardens line the winding streets. Broad Street looks like something from a movie set, perfectly preserved and a reminder of bygone eras.

"Music spills into the streets from live music venues: Golden Era, The Fern, Chief Crazy Horse, The Headquarters, and The Stone House. Even the local radio station KVMR 89.5 FM has music airing outside their studios on the corner of Spring and Bridge Streets."

— MUSE Magazine

The historic Nevada Theatre—the oldest continuously operated theater venue on the West Coast—is home to several companies including Community Asian Theater of the Sierra, Sierra Stages, and The Lyric Rose Theatre Company. On Sundays, The Onyx Downtown screens classic, repertory, and independent films.

Sunday Morning

Explore Nevada City

If you're packing a picnic for a local hike or bike ride along the Pioneer Trail off Highway 20, go to Communal Cafe, Java Johns, or SPD for all the essentials including tasty sandwiches.

Shopping in Nevada City is more like treasure hunting. Field Day sells tailor-made clothing from reclaimed and locally-sourced materials. KitKitDizzi stocks local handmade jewelry and home goods. Check out Holly Dilley's Crush Gallery with its combination of taxidermy and roller-skating items. And no visit is complete without browsing the vintage stores Rainbow Rocket and Solstice.

"Art doesn't just grow here. It thrives and flourishes."

— Jesse Locks, MUSE Magazine

Enjoy a pint from local brewery BrewBilt at their beer garden Brewhaus or Three Forks Brewery and Bakery. If local art with a glass of local wine is more your style, stop into Nevada City Winery or Szabo Winery, where both have monthly exhibitions of local artists.

Both Grass Valley and Nevada City are home to vibrant farmers markets, along with a jam-packed calendar of art walks, street fairs, car shows, brewfests, parades, and film festivals. During the holidays, Grass Valley celebrates its Cornish history with Cornish Christmas, while in Nevada City, Victorian Christmas is a Christmas card come alive.

"There's a very serious creative community around here. There are a lot of amazingly talented and creative people."

— Brian Chambers, The Chambers Project
