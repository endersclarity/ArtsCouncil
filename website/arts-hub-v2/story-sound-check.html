The Story

Lucky Little Towns: A 40-Year Music History

Nevada County's music scene punches above its weight. A rural foothill community that produced Joanna Newsom, a Death Grips cofounder, and a guitarist who joined The Shins. The same network of basement shows, grange halls, and scrappy all-ages venues that launched those careers is still running.

Those of us playing in rock bands in Western Nevada County in the mid-to-late '90s knew we were lucky, and that we owed a debt to the punk bands who'd played here in the decade before. The Circus Tents, Blowhole, Doppelgänger at Seaman's Lodge, the Rough & Ready Grange, and almost constant house parties.

"Those of us playing in rock bands in Western Nevada County in the mid-to-late '90s knew we were lucky."

Adam Kline, Mayor of Nevada City

The next wave brought grunge, pop-punk, and ska. In 1995, Nevada Union High School teacher Loren Brown founded NU Unplugged at the Don Baggett Theatre. Bands like Chode, Badical Turbo Radness, and their contemporaries grew up playing to each other. Then the breakouts started: Hunter joined A.F.I., Jon from the Circus Tents went to The Shins, Zach Hill became the mastermind behind Death Grips. Harpist Joanna Newsom drew inspiration from local minimalist composer Terry Riley and became one of the most respected songwriters of her generation.

Best friends Alela Diane and Mariee Sioux saw Joanna's trajectory and got to work. Bands like Hella, Golden Shoulders, Black Bear, and Casual Fog formed. A local label, Grass Roots Record Company, made it feel official. People elsewhere started noticing something was going on here.

"We're still lucky."

Adam Kline

Today, Aaron Ross and Golden Shoulders remain active. High-energy bands like Beautiful Dudes, Checked-Out, and Earth Exit carry the banner. Singer-songwriters Davia, Graham Farrow, Brett Shady, and Two Runner continue the tradition. From punk basements to concert halls — the scene Adam Kline describes didn't vanish. It just moved into better rooms.
