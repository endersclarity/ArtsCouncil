48 Hours in Truckee

Mountain town, real culture. Skip the ski lodge. Start here.

Share X FB

The jagged peaks are breathtaking, the vistas expansive, and the alpine lakes are cold. In the middle of this beautiful, rugged landscape lies the charming town of Truckee. The town is steeped in history—the Washoe, Maidu, and Paiute Tribes traveled the area centuries before the Donner Party was tragically stranded in a snowstorm during their ill-fated migration west.

Flash forward to today, and the bustling mountain town is a hub for artists, musicians, and creatives, host to several art galleries, public art installations, and live music. During the summer, theatre productions, art, and food events abound, and music echoes in the streets and public parks. In the winter, snow covers the landscape, lights twinkle throughout the town, and a magical wonderland emerges.

Friday Evening

Donner Summit

Heading to Truckee from the west on I-80, stop on Donner Summit and explore the petroglyphs where nomadic tribes etched their art and story into the granite. Then hike up to China Wall, a memorial to the Chinese laborers who were instrumental in creating the Transcontinental Railroad. Walk inside the abandoned train tunnels and appreciate the magnitude of what it took for the railway to move through this area. (Do not attempt this in winter.)

On the way into town, stop at the Donner Memorial State Park Museum. The 1918 Pioneer Monument honors the pioneers who passed through.

Saturday Afternoon

Downtown Art Walk

Stroll the downtown area and explore the town. Riverside Studios is home to beautiful one-of-a-kind handcrafted jewelry, ceramics, and metal art sculpted from lead nails and leatherwork. The Gallery 5830' (named after the elevation of Truckee) features an eclectic mix of artwork. Bespoke and Atelier is a beautiful shop to explore art or purchase art supplies for the inner creative in all of us. Check out Alpenglow Gallery for stunning Tahoe landscape photography.

Public Art & Railroad History

On the other side of the street, the railroad provides a backdrop as trains rumble by. Rusty Ramblers, an ode to the music scene by Anton Standteiner, stands joyfully on the sidewalk. DeRailed and High Iron by Bernie LaForest represent the railroad and its role in bringing commerce to California. The kinetic artist Fred Besch's Locomotion pays homage to Truckee's bike culture. The Train Depot hosts the Mountain Arts Collective, a co-op featuring woodworking, jewelry, weaving, photography, and glasswork.

Saturday Evening

Wine, Workshops & Live Music

Stop in the Pour House, where owners Christa and Dean will regale you in the art of wine. Reserve time to explore the Truckee Roundhouse Maker's Space, where artists offer creative workshops—learn to create shearling boots, butcher block cutting boards, or sock puppets.

"Moody's Bistro Bar and Beats is a must for dinner and live music. It's a happening place where the art of the cocktail, good food, and live music come together."

— Priya Hutner, MUSE Magazine

On the other side of Moody's Bistro, make sure to stop into Lorien Powers' shop featuring stunning jewelry and art. Next door is Piper J Gallery, where art from the region is exhibited.

Sunday Morning

The Other Side of the Tracks

Head to the other side of the tracks. Enjoy a cup of locally roasted java at Dark Horse. Then walk West River Street and view the historic buildings. Stop in Piper J Gallery where art from the region is exhibited.

Before wrapping up the weekend, take a ride around the Truckee Roundabouts, where public art graces the traffic circles. Mountain Flowers by Troy Corliss offers a whimsical moment. The next roundabout features the delightful Truckee Ants, forged by metal worker Matt Parkhurst.

"You may be tired after this 48-hour whirlwind, yet Truckee's art, culture, and beauty will remain embedded in your heart."

— Priya Hutner, MUSE Magazine
