# V1 Discovery Map Brief

Status: internal alignment
Date: 2026-05-06
Source: canonical planning thread, merged with the forked ChatGPT-import thread as source signal

## Summary

V1 is a culture-forward Discovery Map for Nevada County Arts Council / GVNC Cultural District. It should make the map carry the product argument: Nevada County has a rich cultural layer, and the Arts Council is the right narrator of it.

This is not an Arts Hub, a tourism platform, an AI trip planner, a public launch, or a claim to integrate everything. It is an approval-stage product prototype for Arts Council stakeholders to react to before any larger system is defined.

## Product Job

The first job is stakeholder confidence. In the first 30 seconds, Arts Council stakeholders should feel that the direction is culturally credible, ownable, beautiful, useful, and worth continuing.

The real-use audience after that is culture-curious visitors and locals looking for places to go, events worth noticing soon, and simple ways to understand the county's cultural texture.

## Core Experience

- Lead with the map itself: pure map first, with subtle accents rather than a pitch wrapper or guided demo shell.
- Use a refined editorial atlas feel, not a default GIS or generic tourism-board map.
- Show cultural density without pin soup: clusters, counts, featured layers, and zoom reveal can coexist.
- Make Places primary, with Events as a secondary layer that makes the map feel alive.
- Include Paths as curated mapped routes: fixed stops, visible path/route treatment, numbered markers, and light narrative. Do not make Paths dynamic trip planning.
- Provisional V1 Paths: Gallery Walk, Evening in Nevada City, Live Music Night Out.

## Visual And Content Principles

- Align visually with the current Nevada County Arts Council site and Diana-provided NCAC brand guide. The existing asset map UX is not the model, but the surrounding live-site language, red/white/charcoal system, Polymath typography, typemark discipline, hierarchy, and framing principles are source-of-truth constraints.
- Use real images as place proof: actual venues, streets, stages, interiors, murals, art, or cultural spaces. Images should make the map feel tangible and trustworthy, not merely decorative.
- Place detail panels should be desire-first compact cards: image, name, category, short editorial description, then practical links and related events/paths when available.
- Use maintenance-light editorial presentation: evergreen paths, fixed featured anchors, better labels, and MUSE-like copy without creating a weekly content treadmill.
- Use visitor-intent labels backed by data categories. Examples: See a Show, Galleries & Studios, Historic Places, Public Art, Music, Family-Friendly.
- Voice should be MUSE concise: warm, specific, place-aware, practical, and free of tourism hype or institutional jargon.
- Markers should use restrained category icons and hierarchy. Featured/path stops can be larger, numbered, or elevated; regular places stay quieter.

## Scope Boundaries

In scope for V1:

- Interactive map-first discovery surface.
- Cultural places/assets.
- Soon/upcoming events layer.
- Basic modes for Places, Events, and Paths.
- Simple counts, clusters, filters, and featured hints.
- Three curated mapped Paths.
- Visible cleanup of records that appear in first-screen, featured, image-backed, or Path contexts.

Out of scope for V1:

- Public launch before Arts Council approval.
- Public correction/submission CTA.
- Arts Hub or replacement-platform positioning.
- AI-generated itinerary or trip-planning pitch.
- Full event-calendar product.
- Comprehensive data quality audit before alpha.
- Ongoing editorial publishing workflow for Arts Council staff.

## Alpha Success Criteria

Alpha succeeds if Arts Council stakeholders react to the direction and feel with confidence: "Yes, this is ours. This is culturally credible. Keep going."

Primary proof is authority, not feature completeness. V1 should avoid feeling generic first, avoid feeling platform-ish second, and handle data quality through visible cleanup rather than full database perfection.
