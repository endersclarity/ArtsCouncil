Where Art Meets
Leadership—
a Love Letter
to Grass Valley
WORDS / HILARY HODGE,
MAYOR OF GRASS VALLEY

I’ll never forget walking into the first building I saw
at the Nevada County Fairgrounds during WorldFest,
and hearing Perla Batella announce that she was
dedicating her entire set to her favorites from
Leonard Cohen’s catalog. At the first note, I knew
I was home. From concerts to gallery openings,
theatre productions, and more, there is always
something for the arts lover in our community.




Nevada County is a unique place. For such a small community,
we have more than our fair share of talent living locally.
The county’s population is now just over 100,000 people,
while the City of Grass Valley has less than 15,000 residents.
But, I can count at least ten different theatre production
companies, and six community choirs—including a children’s
chorus—in our community. I personally know four people
who make a living designing and building puppets.
 As the Mayor of Grass Valley, I hold the gavel at City Council
 meetings, deliver speeches at ribbon cuttings of new businesses,
 and attend riveting seminars named things like, “The Rising Tide
 of Plastics Litigation.” The usual things any small town Mayor
 might do. But, if you look at my social media profiles, the titles
“Musician, Writer, Poet” precede my more official one.
