                                                         At the heart of every enduring cultural tradition are
                                                         relationships. Since its inception, the Nevada County
                                                         Drum and Dance Collective has been shaped by
                                                         dedicated individuals who together have created
                                                         a welcoming, intergenerational space to explore
                                                         the traditions of the African Diaspora.
                                                         Over the years, the collective has built lasting relationships with
                                                         master teachers—many of whom return annually—and artists
                                                         who have shared their talents here, like Youssouff Koumbassa,
                                                         Djeneba Sako, Mandjou Kone, Mariam Diakite, Arnaud Loubayi,
                                                         Mama Mabiba, Moussa Camara, Susana Arenas, Kimberly
                                                         Miguel-Mullen, Muisi-Kongo Malonga, and Wilfred Marks.
                                                         The story begins in 1980, when Nevada City resident Cai Sorlien
                                                         met Congolese masters Titos Sompa and Malonga Casquelourd
                                                         in East Palo Alto. They had brought the powerful traditions of their

                                                         In the early 2000s, former recording studio and record label,
                                                         Kanaga System Krush Records, brought local dancers and
                                                         drummers to Mali to study in village settings with master artists,
                                                         which brought home unique experiences that strengthened the
                                                         community’s connection to these traditions.
                                                         Born from these connections, in 2015, Seydou Dante—a griot
                                                         from Mali—moved to Nevada County, and immediately became
                                                         the heart of the collective. His weekly classes share polyrhythms,
                                                         such as Dansa, Mendiani, Sunun, and Madan, each rooted in
                                                         cultural meaning and used to mark life’s transitions.
                                                         Currently calling the Miners Foundry Cultural Center home,
                                                         the collective’s near 40-year tradition continues to thrive—
                                                         celebrating rhythm, culture, and the joyful power of human
