Off The Wall:
Explorations
of Art In Nature
   WORDS / ANNETTE MULLER


Each June, on trails across Nevada County,
stars sway from low branches, catching the
breeze and the eyes of passersby. These stars,
with their vibrant scenes and woven patterns,
are the work of artists from Neighborhood
Center of the Arts, created for Bear Yuba Land
Trust’s annual Summer Star Hike Challenge.
The stars are a product of the organization’s
commissioning of local artists to create place-
specific artworks on public trust-held lands.
With over 50 miles of trails and 26,000 acres
conserved, that’s quite the canvas.
