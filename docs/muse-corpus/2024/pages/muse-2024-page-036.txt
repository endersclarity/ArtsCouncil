In 2007, filmmaker Mike Mills was the
spotlight director at the 7th Annual Nevada
City Film Festival. At the time he was best
known as a graphic designer and music
video director who had created ground
breaking videos for Moby, Yoko Ono and
Air, before launching a successful film
career that would include award-winning
films like Beginners, 20th Century Women
and C’mon, C’mon.

While graciously answering questions from the audience,
he shared that during college he’d learned to edit using a
software program called “Grass Valley.” With a laugh he said
that never in his wildest dreams did he think it was named
for a small town in the foothills of Northern California, let
alone, twenty years later he’d find himself in the product’s
