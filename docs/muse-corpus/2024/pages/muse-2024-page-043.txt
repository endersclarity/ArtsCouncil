      agehen is a place where layers of stories tell the stories
      of their own becoming. A Proving Ground began
      with an art and science experiment proposed by
the seminal ecological artists, Helen and Newton Harrison
at UC Berkeley Sagehen Field Station in 2013.

The Harrisons were looking for a place to test their ideas
about the resiliency of plant guilds in the Sierras, and were
invited to Sagehen, where they established plots at five
altitudes and began growing out seeds harvested from there
to see what heroic plant guilds might emerge as a warming
climate reduced ice and snowpack over time. That search
revealed an overlapping series of stories, about people,
place and the nature of landscape.
