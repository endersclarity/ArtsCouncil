# Arts Council Demo Brief

Status: working brief
Date: 2026-05-24

## One-Line Direction

The next V1 Discovery Map demo should prove cultural authority: NCAC can explain why a place matters, not merely show that it has coordinates and a website.

## What To Show First

Open with the live V1 Discovery Map and one polished Cultural Anchor card.

Suggested first card: The Center for the Arts.

Say:

> This is not a finished public launch or a completed countywide data cleanup. It is a focused GVNC prototype slice showing how NCAC can turn vetted cultural asset data into an authored discovery experience.

## How To Explain The 6+4 Shape

The six primary anchors are not a ranking of cultural worth. They are the first polished proof objects for the demo.

Four supporting stops remain part of the story and can support routes/layers without carrying equal primary-card weight:

- Nevada City Winery
- ASiF Studios
- Hirschman Trail / Hirschman's Pond
- North Columbia Schoolhouse Cultural Center

Say:

> All 1,000+ places remain valid NCAC cultural assets. The anchor cards are where we add authored treatment first so reviewers can judge the experience before we scale the pattern.

## Phase Plan

### Phase 1: Prove The Anchor Card Pattern

Use the six primary cards to show that the map can feel intentional, local, and culturally specific.

Success signal: Eliza/Diane understand why these cards feel different from generic map listings.

### Phase 2: Connect Anchors To Paths

Use the 6+4 set to make paths feel authored rather than algorithmic.

Success signal: reviewers can see a route as a cultural story, not a trip-planner output.

### Phase 3: Add MUSE Carefully

Use MUSE as voice everywhere, but explicit MUSE claims only where local data supports a match.

Success signal: the map borrows NCAC editorial authority without pretending every place has a MUSE article.

### Phase 4: Decide What Future Mockups Are Needed

Only after the live prototype is reviewed, use labeled image-generation concept mockups for gaps or optional directions.

Good candidates:

- missing-image treatment for non-primary records
- richer MUSE story layer
- Culture Forward / stakeholder lens

## What Not To Claim

- Do not claim the full 1,000+ record dataset is finished.
- Do not claim every anchor is a MUSE place.
- Do not present generated images as documentary place photos.
- Do not reopen Truckee or countywide scope for this demo.
- Do not use internal language such as P0, confidence, implementation priority, or demo tier.

## Decision Ask

Ask reviewers:

> Does this anchor-card pattern feel like NCAC owning cultural discovery?

If yes, the next build step is expanding the pattern deliberately: first through paths, then through story/event relationships, then through broader dataset cleanup.
